Our Mission

In late 2012 two college students, by the names of Ethan muchnik and Harsh
Tiwary both realized that the environment around them was being destroyed by
pollution from gas guzzling cars. After years of watching the world indecisively
tackle the problem, they decided to help the environment themselves by making Lightwave:
an electric car company. From the beginning it was the company's stated goal to make modern
electric vehicles affordable to the masses.

Of course the first step was making an actual car. After 2 years of research into how to make the ultimate
electric car, Lightwave released its first car to the world: Dune. This was a truly revolutionary Sedan
which offered an unprecedented 300 miles of range for an electric car. The people of America clearly
liked the car as they brought over 10,000 models by 2015, only a year after the car's
release.

Of course, Ethan and Harsh knew that to truly give every American a new car,
they would have to compete in all the car categories. In March of 2016, two new cars were
announced by Lightwave, Alpine and Taiga which were a pickup truck and an SUV. Due to the recognition
that Lightwave got for Dune, Alpine and Taiga sold out even quicker. By the end of 2016 each of
Lightwave’s cars got approximately 150,000 orders. People were truly astounded on how modern, safe,
and efficient the cars were. Some newspapers commented that the cars were essentially going at the
speed of a wave of light.

Through most of 2017, Lightwave focused on two projects that were made
public, the building of its immense charging network and the updating of the cars through
software updates. In early August Lightwave showed the world River and Waterfall.
Waterfall was a fast charger that could charge up any car in Lightwave’s lineup in less than 15 minutes for
free. Meanwhile River was the slower yet more numerous charger. Nevertheless the River could still
charge up an electric car in less than 45 minutes. In October, Lightwave released new LightVision
Safety features that ensured that every car would have the functionality to automatically brake
while also being able to drive on highways. This update was hailed as potentially life saving and
revolutionary.

At the end of 2017 Lightwave announced a surprise presentation. Many believed it would
be a new update or some new colors added, but Lightwave had something much more revolutionary in mind.
At this presentation Lightwave announced Neptune, the sports car and Boulder a semi-truck.
Neptune was flat out the fastest 0-60 car ever announced beating the previous record by nearly 10%.
Meanwhile, boulder had the biggest electric motor in any car or truck. Many hailed this as
the last step that was needed so that everybody could buy an electric car.

While theLightwave team knows that its announcement was revolutionary we all know that we have more to do
in order to save our planet by making electric cars.We appreciate your interest and we hope that
you will join our cause.
